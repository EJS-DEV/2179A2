Why It Matters Where You Live

The technology used to connect your home to the NBN significantly impacts performance. Fiber-to-the-premises delivers the best results, while fiber-to-the-node faces more constraints.

By connection type: FTTP delivers 99.8% of advertised speeds while FTTN (copper) only achieves 94.3%, a 5.5% gap that means 50-100 Mbps real-world difference.

By speed quality: Most Australian areas receive adequate or high-speed service, but a significant portion remain under-served with speeds below 25 Mbps.

By latency quality: The majority of locations enjoy excellent or good ping times, though some areas experience latency issues affecting gaming and video calls.

Chapter 5

The Evening Slowdown

Network congestion increases significantly during evening busy hours (7pm-11pm). See how different plan types are affected throughout the day.

Average Speeds by Hour of Day
Plan Tiers:

Loading time-of-day data...

The evening slowdown is real, and it's more pronounced for higher-tier plans. While NBN 25 maintains roughly 95% of its advertised speed throughout the day, NBN 1000 users see their speeds drop from 82% in the early morning to just 72% during peak evening hours (8-10pm). This congestion effect means premium plan users experience a greater reduction in speeds when network demand peaks. The busiest period is 8-9pm when streaming, gaming, and video calls converge.
